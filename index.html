Passings

Wednesday, 18 March 2026

Dear CVNet colleagues,

Two years ago, during a discussion on CVNet about which deceased vision scientists should be remembered and documented, Lothar Spillmann undertook the ambitious task of compiling a near-comprehensive list. Over the intervening period, he and several colleagues have worked extensively to assemble a remarkable resource: a list of deceased vision scientists accompanied by brief biographical notes.

We are very pleased to make this work available to the community. The document is now available on the CVNet website as a PDF, exactly as submitted to us. The PDF can be downloaded here.

Soon, we will also release an online version of the list. This version will allow entries to be indexed and browsed in multiple ways—for example, by alphabetical order, year of birth, year of death, and other useful categories, in addition to the chapters included in Lothar’s original list—making it easier to explore the history of our field.

If you notice errors or omissions, or have suggestions for additions or corrections, please feel free to send them to Christopher Tyler and David Peterzell, who are coordinating CVNet updates for this project. This is intended to be a living document that will be refined and expanded over time.

We are grateful to Lothar and his collaborators for the considerable effort that went into assembling this valuable historical resource.

Best regards,
David Peterzell
(for CVNet)

Recent Passings

Ariane Lévy-Schoen (1927–2026)
Date of Passing
March 23, 2026
Affiliation
Laboratoire de Psychologie Expérimentale, CNRS, Université René Descartes (EPHE, EHESS), Paris
Description
A leading figure in French vision science and Research Director at the CNRS, she trained in psychology, physics, and mathematics in Strasbourg, Paris, and New York. Her early research on children's perception of facial expressions earned her a Doctorate in Letters from the University of Paris (L'image d'autrui chez l'enfant, 1964). She then investigated active visual perception and visuomotor space in children and adults (with J. Blanc-Garin, J. Paillard, and V. Pouthas), before turning fully to eye movement control with L'Étude des mouvements oculaires (1969) and foundational work on saccadic programming (e.g., using simultaneous and sequential double stimulation - L’année Psychologique,1969, and with Blanc-Garin, Brain Research,1974). In the mid-1970s, with J. K. O'Regan, she revived research on eye movements in reading. They introduced the "optimal viewing position" concept (with J. Pynte and B. Brugaillère, 1984) and co-authored the strategy-and-tactics theory (Attention and Performance XII, 1987), which proposed an adaptive interplay between visuomotor and linguistic factors that shaped generations of reading models. They also co-founded the 'Groupe Regard,' a major European hub for eye movement research that trained researchers including C. Coëffé, A. Jacobs, T. Nazir, Z. Kapoula, F. Vitu, and R. Humbert, and fostered lasting collaborations with J. Findlay, H. Deubel, J. Pynte, and artists F. and V. Molnar. Co-edited (with O'Regan) Eye Movements: From Physiology to Cognition (1987), proceedings of the Third European Conference on Eye Movements (Dourdan, 1985).
